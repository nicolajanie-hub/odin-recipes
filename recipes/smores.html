Recipe for Smores

Description

S'mores are a classic campfire food that is eternally popular with kids in the USA. Not everyone likes them; some people find them too sickly sweet, but I personally think they're fun and a nice treat to have on occasion.

However, there is a problem with s'mores: traditionally, they require a campfire to make, and s'mores purists will tell you that there is no way to make s'mores without the campfire. I will concede that roasting the marshmallows over the campfire adds something to the recipe, whether that something is from enjoying time spent with friends and/or family, or the slight crunchiness of a marshmallow that was fully on fire for a moment.

But realistically: when I want to have s'mores, I don't always have the ability to access a campfire. So, this is how I go about making s'mores when I can't or don't want to use a fire to do so.

Ingredients

  • Graham Crackers (You can experiment with different types of graham crackers, or with other kinds of cookies)
  • Chocolate Bar (Ideally one that is relatively flat; you can also experiment with chocolate with different kinds of filling)
  • Marshmallows (Again, you can experiment with different flavors, and you should be able to use vegan marshmallows if you would like)
  • Optional: Peanut Butter, Nutella, or other spreads

Basic Steps

  1. Split one graham cracker into two squares. If you have a spread you want to include, you should spread it on one of the graham crackers now as well.
  2. Break off a piece of the chocolate bar. The exact number of sections you use will vary depending on the type of chocolate bar you are using, but you generally will want the piece you break off to be just a little smaller than the graham cracker squares.
  3. Decide if you want to use one or two marshmallows. This is mostly up to personal taste.
  4. Heat the marshmallows. You can do this in a microwave, but be warned that the marshmallow won't really get "toasted" and will expand significantly; if you pick the microwave, don't microwave the marshmallow for longer than 30 seconds. You may be able to toast the marshmallow in an air fryer, oven, or toaster oven instead if you would like to get closer to what a traditional s'mores is like, but I have not tried that myself so I can't give advice on it.
  5. Put the chocolate bar on one graham cracker square, then the marshmallow. Then, you can put the other graham cracker on top of the marshmallow, press it down, and you're done! The marshmallow and melting chocolate should be enough for the s'mores to stick together even if you aren't using some kind of spread.

Additional Notes

  • If you leave a marshmallow in the microwave for too long, it will start to crystallize. A hard marshmallow is the exact opposite of what you want for s'mores; you want the marshmallow to be soft and melty.
  • No matter how you choose to heat the marshmallow, you NEED to keep a close eye on it! You will want to make sure that you pull it out of whatever you are using to heat it before it burns. Like I said, I have always used the microwave in the past, so there may be dangers to other methods that I am not aware of; I would recommend if you plan to use a different method, you look into it a little more and see if you can find other recipes that use that method.
